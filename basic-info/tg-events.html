Temple Guard Tryouts

The Temple Guard Tryouts are designed to assess the potential and readiness of new recruits. The process includes:

  • Strike System & Background Check: The host describes the three-strike system and performs a background check using the !profile command.
  • Formations Test: Attendees are tested on formations. Mistakes or delays result in strikes.
  • Dueling: Attendees duel with the host or assisting guards, showing potential without strikes.
  • Questioning: Attendees answer a series of questions and scenarios. Inadequate answers or cheating results in strikes.
  • Oath of Genesis: Successful attendees recite the Oath of Genesis with the host.

Patrols

Patrols involve a group of Temple Guards protecting the temple under a host's supervision. Duties may include:

  • Defending against raids.
  • Following host commands diligently.
  • Additional duties as specified in the Guarding Information section.

Induction Ceremonies

Induction Ceremonies are mandatory for progressing past the rank of Student, and are run on a two-strike system:

  • Questions: Inductees are questioned about the Temple Guard. Answers must be clear and precise.
  • General Rules Recitation: Inductees must recite 'General Rules' in their own words. Copying results in failure.
  • Duels: Inductees demonstrate proficiency in three duels, utilizing AC and GB with good movement.
  • Induction Ceremony Oath: Successful inductees recite the Induction Ceremony Oath.
  • Jail System Knowledge: Inductees learn about the jail system and associated rules and commands.

Warden Evaluations

Warden Evaluations are crucial for Vanguards seeking to advance and follow a two-strike system:

  • Eligibility: Only off-trial Captains may host. Vanguards must be strike-free for 2 weeks.
  • Dueling: The attendee duels the host 5 times. If deemed skillful, the evaluation proceeds.
  • Testing: Attendees are tested on faces, chains, formations, and scenarios relevant to events.
  • Reattempts: Failed evaluations require a 2-day wait before reattempting. Multiple failures increase the cooldown period by 1 day.
  • High Ranks: High Ranks cannot host evaluations for Vanguards they trained within SWU.

Trainings

Basic Trainings

Basic Trainings are mandatory for all guards to progress and include:

  • General rules of the division.
  • Lines of engagement.
  • Guarding positions.
  • Grammar jacks and Hell jacks.
  • Mandatory 5-question quiz.

Faces and Formations Trainings

These trainings enhance rigidity and command-following abilities and may include:

  • Faces.
  • Chains.
  • Formations.
  • Marching Formations.
  • Knockout competitions for speed and accuracy.

Combat Trainings

Combat Trainings improve combat skills and may feature:

  • Mini-games like Health Rotations, Gladiators, and Juggernaut.
  • One-on-one dueling with saber and Force.
  • Practical feedback.
  • Battle scenarios for guarding the Temple.

Special Events

Tournament of the Guard (TOTG)

A monthly event to determine the best duelist, split into 2 timezones:

  • Winners decide to split the Robux reward or duel for a larger prize pool.

Mass Patrols

Weekly events with a High Command member present:

  • Any Warden(+) can request to host but must pre-plan with the High Command Team.
  • Usually accompanied by a counter-patrol from the Taskforce division.

Public Trainings

TJO-wide events hosted by High Command members to give non-members insight:

  • Review faces, formations, and combat from a Temple Guard perspective.
  • Teamwork-focused combat through Gladiators, Health Rotations, and Raid Simulations.
  • Opportunity for prospective guards to learn and gain attention.
